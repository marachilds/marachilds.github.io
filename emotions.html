A 2:30 motion graphics piece that strengthens emotional literacy by introducing people to the Plutchik Wheel of Emotions

A school project called Moving Information, completed individually. October 2017.

Adobe After Effects + Illustrator

Initial Exploration

Given that the subject of information is endlessly broad, I began by choosing an initial goal for this project: to make people think. As I'm fascinated with what it is that makes us distinctly human, I have become more and more interested in emotional literacy, the ability to understand your emotions, empathize with others', and express them productively. From there, I decided to research The Plutchik Wheel of Emotions, a widely used emotional model.

Visual Identity

I developed the visual identity of this around a clean, bright tone. The League Spartan typeface is used for the commentary and "friendlier" pieces of information, while Courier New remains sterile and academic in tone. The contrast between the black and white is increased by the use of colors when emotions are highlighted.

"Storyboarding"

Rather than creating a traditional frame-oriented storyboard, I used the fact that my piece was going to be text-centered to develop a script much like a movie script. I included the main animations necessary as well as the transitions. It seemed unnecessary to repeatedly draw the same visual element, the wheel, especially when it had already been created.

reflection

Having just touched the surface of emotional literacy, this project helped me see that I really enjoy creating things with the intent to aid and educate. I've also enjoyed exploring the concept of emotion classification and finding out about how emotions affect human behavior. Animating is also something that is detail-oriented, from individual keyframing to transition flow, and I'm interested in getting better at it.
